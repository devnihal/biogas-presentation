Problem Statement

  • India generates over 65 million tonnes of food waste annually, with significant contributions from restaurants and canteens
  • Mismanagement of food waste leads to methane emissions, exacerbating climate change
  • Commercial kitchens face rising LPG fuel costs, increasing operational expenses
  • There is an urgent need for sustainable solutions that convert waste into renewable energy

Project Objectives

  • Convert organic kitchen waste into biogas as a clean cooking fuel alternative
  • Assess the technical and economic feasibility for Indian restaurants and canteens
  • Promote sustainable waste management while reducing fossil fuel dependency
  • Explore scalability potential aligned with Indian government policies

Biogas Plant Design (India)

  • Common designs include fixed-dome and KVIC floating drum digesters adapted for India
  • Main components: waste feed inlet, digester tank, gas storage dome, hydrogen sulfide scrubber, and slurry outlet
  • Typical scale: 2 to 10 cubic meters of digester volume
  • Constructed using bricks or ferrocement for cost-effectiveness and durability

Feedstock & Biogas Yield

  • Indian restaurant food waste typically contains cooked rice, mixed vegetables, dals, and peels
  • Expected biogas yield: 0.3 to 0.35 m¬≥ per kilogram of food waste
